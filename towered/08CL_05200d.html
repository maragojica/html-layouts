Name: Chiang Mai Virtual Exchange Program

Description

This program will adequately prepare students in the areas of economics, philosophy, politics, religion, culture, and language of Thailand and other Southeast Asian countries.

Program Information Academics Logistics and Expenses Program Summary
Program Summary
Program Name:
South Asian Economics, Politics, and Culture Studies
Program Description:
The Chiang Mai program is an opportunity to live and study in a lively, vibrant city in one of the most scenic areas of an already beautiful country. The curriculum integrates multiple disciplines in order to respond to the ever-changing issues and events surrounding the country and region. The program is ideal for students interested in economics, philosophy, politics, religions, culture, and language of Thailand and other Southeast Asian countries.
Instructor Name:
Denaye Thompson
Instructor Bio:
Denaye Thompson is an Associate Professor of Cultural Studies at Charles Sturt University. She received her BA from AHEA University and her Ph.D. in East Asian Studies from Charles Sturt University. Denaye has authored over 50 peer-reviewed publications and written several books focused on the history of east Asia. Denaye received the distinguished lecturer award from Charles Sturt University in 2021.
Instructor Photo:
Courses:
  • Introduction to International Business
  • Southeast Asian Economics, Politics, and Culture Studies
  • Asian Traditions
  • Southeast Asian History from 1800 to Present
Learning Outcomes:
  • Students will be able to demonstrate an understanding of regional tourism, management, trade, and economics.
  • Students will be able to immerse themselves in international logistics, communication, society, and culture.
Program Benefits:
  • Students will interact with business professionals from the Chiang Mai area to gain first-hand experience with international trade and Thailand's business culture.
  • Students will also have the opportunity to tour cultural heritage sites virtually with a local guide to better appreciate Thailand's history and the importance of tourism to the economy.

  • Welcome to InspirED ™

    The InspirED™ platform is designed to support the critical mission and goals of higher education: teaching and learning, institutional effectiveness, student engagement and outcomes, growth strategies, curriculum development, student mobility, faculty development, efficiency, and cost containment. In this release, we are introducing our professional engagement solution, CollaboratED™. Explore the platform by reviewing your home tiles and left navigation menu items.

  • Update your Profile.

    Select your profile photo or name in the top navigation and select My Profile to view your profile. Select Edit Profile to begin updating your profile. Here you will see the information you included in your AHEA Membership Application. As you match with other prospective partners, they will be able to view your profile and invite you to their projects. Be sure to keep your profile updated to optimize your CollaboratED™ experience.

  • Add Interests.

    When editing your profile, scroll down to the Collaboration Interests tile and select the Add button to create your first interest. Interests will help you find better matches in the platform. CollaboratED™ will provide you with recommendations that pair well with your interests.

  • Meet your Matches.

    After creating your interests, select the Find Matches button on the Profile screen or while editing your interest to find prospective partners. On the Matches screen, you can browse through prospects, view their profiles, send them messages, and invite them to projects.

  • Create Projects.

    CollaboratED™ is designed to guide you through Course and Certificate Development Projects. Create projects from your interests on the profile screen, or start a new project on the My Projects screen. While in the project space, complete tasks to advance to milestones. Invite additional partners while you work. Add files and send messages to partners for effective collaboration. Stay tuned as we expand our project offerings to peer reviews, virtual exchange programs, and more.

  • Utilize Resources.

    Discover techniques, templates, and tutorials that will help with optimizing the use of InspirED™, to manage the academic lifecycle at your institution, to collaborate with peers, and to engage constituents. Navigate to the Resources section by selecting Resources in your left navigation menu. The American Higher Education Alliance has constructed templates to support curriculum development, campus programs, and strategic initiatives.

  • Engage in Forum Discussions.

    You have full access to the American Higher Education Alliance network of instructors, administrators, and industry experts. Visit the Forums to discuss leading industry topics and to create some of your own. Go to Forums by selecting Forums in your left navigation menu. Meet AHEA Forum Moderators, share your expertise, and learn from others in your field.
