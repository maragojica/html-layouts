Add an Interest and Review Matches

As a faculty member you can brainstorm, share ideas, and collaborate with other academic professionals using the InspirED™ platform. This guide will give you step-by-step instructions on how to add interests to your profile. It will also explain how the platform matches you with other users who share similar interests. This way you can begin collaborating right away, both locally and abroad.

Let's start with the matching process. To begin, InspirED recommends project partners based on the information gathered from user profiles. It does this by sorting through key fields within the platform including study area, delivery method, location, and language. But before the matching process can begin, you must complete the “Collaboration Interests” section of your profile.

Step 1: Collaboration Interests Tile

In your profile is the “Collaboration Interests” tile. This is where you post the types of projects you’re interested in collaborating on with others. Simply click the Edit Profile button in the top right corner of your Personal Information tile. Then scroll to the bottom right corner of your profile to find the “Collaboration Interests” tile. Here you will create a new interest for every project you want to undertake. The information you enter into the form will help InspirED match you with other like-minded individuals.

Step 2: Add Interest Form

Click the Add button in the “Collaboration Interests” tile to bring up the “Add Interest” form. You will need to populate the following fields to create a Collaboration Interest of your own:

  • Project Type
  • Description
  • Preferred Language
  • Collaboration Type
  • Study Area 1 (choose up to 3)
  • Region 1 (choose up to 4)
  • Project Date Range
Step 3: Select a Project

The first field allows you to select the project type that interests you. In this example, we’ll select Course Development.

  • Academic Journal
  • Best Practices
  • Certificate Development
  • Course Development
  • Curriculum Development
  • General Publication
  • Mentorship
  • Peer Review
  • Research
  • Study Abroad
  • Other
Step 4: Description

The Description text box is where you provide additional information about your project. There is a limit to the number of characters allowed so be sure your entry is clear and concise. The description box appears under all project types. After you fill out your description, move to the next field to enter your Preferred Language.

Step 5: Preferred Language

The Preferred Language dropdown lists a variety of languages. Choose the one that makes you feel most comfortable when communicating with students, faculty, and affiliates. Keep in mind, you can only select one language.

  • Arabic
  • Bengali
  • English
  • French
  • Hindi
  • Japanese
  • Mandarin
  • Norwegian
  • Punjabi
  • Russian
  • Spanish
Step 6: Collaboration Type

When it comes to selecting the type of collaboration you want to engage in, you may find potential partners around the corner or around the globe. Select your preference from the following options:

  • Departmental - Partners within your specific department
  • Institutional - Partners within your institution
  • Regional - Partners located in your country
  • Global – Partners located around the world
Step 7: Review Region

If you choose departmental, institutional, or regional from the “Collaboration Type” dropdown, your region will auto populate. This is because the location is pulled from your institution’s profile information within InspirED. In the example below, North America appears in the Region 1 field because the user’s institution is located in the United States.

If you choose “Global” as your Collaboration Type, you may select either “All” in the Region 1 field or up two four regions in the Region 1 – Region 4 dropdowns.

  • All
  • Africa
  • Asia
  • Central America
  • Europe
  • Middle East
  • North America
  • Oceania
  • South America
  • The Caribbean
Step 8: Delivery Method (Select Project Types)

Delivery Method displays the Course Development project type and provides options for Blended or Hybrid, Online, and On-Ground.

Step 9: Subject Area

The Subject Area field is where you select the subject or subjects related to your interest. Read below for the full list of subject areas available. You can select up to three.

  • Anthropology
  • Archaeology
  • Arts
  • Biology
  • Business
  • Chemistry
  • Computer Science
  • Earth Science
  • Economics
  • Education
  • Engineering
  • History
  • Human Geography
  • Languages
  • Law
  • Literature
  • Mathematics
  • Medicine and Health
  • Philosophy
  • Physics
  • Political Science
  • Psychology
  • Sociology
  • Space Sciences
  • Statistics
  • Theology
Step 10: Program Length

Program Length is an option under Course Development. Here you have a choice between the following options.

  • Mini Term
  • Ongoing
  • Quarter
  • Semester
  • Summer
  • Trimester
Step 11: Credits (Select Project Types)

The Course Development Project Type gives you the option to choose from a dropdown with 1, 2, 3, 4, or Other Credits.

  • Mini Term
  • Ongoing
  • Quarter
  • Semester
  • Summer
  • Trimester
Step 12: Program Level (Select Project Types)

When you’re working on a Course Development or Curriculum Development, you will be able to choose the Program Level from the following options:

  • Certificate
  • Associates
  • Undergraduate
  • Graduate
  • Masters
  • Doctoral
Step 13: Project Date Range

The Project Date Range lets you indicate the span of time you anticipate your project will last. Click in each field to open the calendar associated with that field. Then select the Start Date and End Date of your project.

Step 14: Save Interest

Once you have populated all the required fields in the “Add Interests” form, click Save. Your new interest displays in the Collaboration Interests tile within the Edit Profile screen. To view your new interest on your Profile, click Save Profile and the system will return you to your profile. Your newly created interest will display in the Collaboration Interests tile and is available for other users to view.

Step 15: Review New Interest

Once you have created your new interest, you can review all the details by clicking View More in the new tile.

The selected interest form displays with the information provided and the date it was created. Please note that you may see fewer fields based on the project type you choose. As you see in this example, Course Development contains all the fields we have discussed in this guide.

Step 16: Edit Interest

Now that you’ve created a new project interest, it’s time to review and edit it, if necessary. To do this, enter the Edit Profile screen and click View More on the interest you want to revise. The Interest modal will pop on the screen. If you see information you want to change, select the Edit button.

The Edit Interest form will appear. This gives you the opportunity to change the information in any field. Once you’ve made your corrections, click the Save button.

Step 17: View Recommended Partners

Now that you’ve set up your first Interest in the system, you will want to find project partners to collaborate with. To do this, click Home in the left navigation menu.

This will take you to your Home screen where you will find “Recommended Partners.” This tile shows a truncated list of potential project partners that match the criteria you set as part of your Areas of Interest.

Click View Matches.

Step 18: View Matches

Once you select the View Matches button, you will be taken to the “Matching” screen where you can see the full list of recommended partners.

Here you will find the profile pictures, names, titles, departments, institutions, and locations of members who share similar interests to yours.

Step 19: Match Profile

If you see someone who interests you, click on the recommended partner’s name to view their profile screen. From this screen, you can see their personal information, institution profile, communication preferences, credentials, professional bio, and collaboration interests.

Once you find other academic professionals who match your requirements, you’ll be able to brainstorm, share ideas, and collaborate.

Now that’s InspirED!

  • Welcome to InspirED ™

    The InspirED™ platform is designed to support the critical mission and goals of higher education: teaching and learning, institutional effectiveness, student engagement and outcomes, growth strategies, curriculum development, student mobility, faculty development, efficiency, and cost containment. In this release, we are introducing our professional engagement solution, CollaboratED™. Explore the platform by reviewing your home tiles and left navigation menu items.

  • Update your Profile.

    Select your profile photo or name in the top navigation and select My Profile to view your profile. Select Edit Profile to begin updating your profile. Here you will see the information you included in your AHEA Membership Application. As you match with other prospective partners, they will be able to view your profile and invite you to their projects. Be sure to keep your profile updated to optimize your CollaboratED™ experience.

  • Add Interests.

    When editing your profile, scroll down to the Collaboration Interests tile and select the Add button to create your first interest. Interests will help you find better matches in the platform. CollaboratED™ will provide you with recommendations that pair well with your interests.

  • Meet your Matches.

    After creating your interests, select the Find Matches button on the Profile screen or while editing your interest to find prospective partners. On the Matches screen, you can browse through prospects, view their profiles, send them messages, and invite them to projects.

  • Create Projects.

    CollaboratED™ is designed to guide you through Course and Certificate Development Projects. Create projects from your interests on the profile screen, or start a new project on the My Projects screen. While in the project space, complete tasks to advance to milestones. Invite additional partners while you work. Add files and send messages to partners for effective collaboration. Stay tuned as we expand our project offerings to peer reviews, virtual exchange programs, and more.

  • Utilize Resources.

    Discover techniques, templates, and tutorials that will help with optimizing the use of InspirED™, to manage the academic lifecycle at your institution, to collaborate with peers, and to engage constituents. Navigate to the Resources section by selecting Resources in your left navigation menu. The American Higher Education Alliance has constructed templates to support curriculum development, campus programs, and strategic initiatives.

  • Engage in Forum Discussions.

    You have full access to the American Higher Education Alliance network of instructors, administrators, and industry experts. Visit the Forums to discuss leading industry topics and to create some of your own. Go to Forums by selecting Forums in your left navigation menu. Meet AHEA Forum Moderators, share your expertise, and learn from others in your field.
